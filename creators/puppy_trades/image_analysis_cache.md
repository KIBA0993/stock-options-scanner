### Recurring Chart Patterns

**Elliott Wave Analysis**
The trader employs Elliott Wave Theory as their primary visual pattern recognition methodology:
- Identifies complete 5-wave impulse sequences (trending moves)
- Maps 3-wave corrective patterns (ABC corrections)
- Uses wave counting to determine current position within larger cycle structure
- Projects future price targets based on wave relationships and expected wave completion
- Focuses on identifying wave 3 and wave 5 positions (strongest trending waves)

**Multi-Year Technical Structure**
- Uses extended timeframes (monthly charts, 30-year views) for major trend analysis
- Incorporates RSI indicator in lower panel for momentum confirmation
- Marks specific price targets derived from wave projections
- Uses projection lines to visualize anticipated price trajectory

### Timeframe Preferences

**Long-term positioning:** Monthly charts spanning multiple decades (e.g., 2003-2031 view on IBM). This suggests the trader uses visual Elliott Wave analysis for identifying multi-year position trades rather than short-term swings.

### Transferable Methodology (Non-Crypto)

**Elliott Wave forecasting on traditional equities:** The IBM analysis demonstrates the trader applies the same wave-counting methodology to blue-chip stocks as they would to any asset. The technique involves:
1. Identifying completed wave sequences to determine current cycle position
2. Counting impulse (1-2-3-4-5) and corrective (A-B-C) waves using color-coded annotations
3. Projecting future price movement based on wave theory relationships
4. Setting specific price targets at anticipated wave completion points
5. Using multi-year timeframes to capture complete wave cycles

**Contrarian sentiment analysis through market narratives:** Beyond pure technical patterns, the trader monitors when market participants mindlessly repeat buzzword narratives (e.g., "crypto is an AI play") as potential signals of excessive speculation, groupthink, or market exhaustion. This represents a visual/qualitative pattern recognition of euphoric market psychology.
