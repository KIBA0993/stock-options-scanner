## Trading Personality

@kpak82 is a strictly technical, contrarian swing trader who specializes in identifying major reversal points at price extremes — both tops and bottoms. He is blunt, evidence-based (self-described medical background), and openly contemptuous of momentum-chasing and FOMO buying. He is not a permabear: he called the March 2026 bottom bullishly and shifted bearish only when multi-timeframe resistance converged. ## Market Conditions They Trade

- **Parabolic exhaustion**: Markets that have gone up 4–9+ consecutive weeks/days in "historic" fashion, especially when accompanied by extreme RSI readings, no pullback to key EMAs, and broad sentiment euphoria
- **Multi-timeframe resistance convergence**: When monthly, weekly, and daily charts all simultaneously hit channel tops, trend line resistance, Fibonacci levels, or EMA clusters
- **VIX at extreme oversold**: When VIX RSI hits levels seen only 5–6 times historically, with bullish divergence setting up
- **Post-V-rally reversal zones**: After a fast, near-vertical recovery (e.g., +30% in 6 weeks from crash lows) where RSI divergence appears and price stalls

## Market Conditions They Avoid

- **Straight-line parabolic rallies mid-move**: Explicitly states "technicals are useless" when market "chops or extends into extreme, low probability historic rallies" — does not chase entries mid-extension

- **Day-trading**: "Daytrading is hard b/c it's like trying to figure out where an infant that just woke up from a nap is going next" — not his domain
- **10th+ consecutive week higher**: Explicitly computes historical probability as <1–2%; will not go long at that point

## Setup Triggers — What Gets Their Attention

**Primary trigger: Multi-element convergence at extremes.** A setup becomes high-conviction only when multiple independent elements stack at the same price zone simultaneously. Specific elements he watches:

1. **Channel resistance (multi-timeframe)**: Lifetime/quarterly/10–50 year channels hitting at the same time as shorter-term channels (e.g., "$MU — top of lifetime channel range" + "$AMD — top of 50-year lifetime channel range")
2. **Bearish reversal candlesticks** at those resistance zones: bearish engulfing, shooting star, gravestone doji, hanging man, dark cloud cover, harami cross — specifically calling out that gravestone doji has "much higher success rate as a reversal candle than shooting stars"
3. **Negative RSI divergence**: Price at new highs but RSI making lower high — especially powerful when prior RSI peak was "5–7 months apart." Calls RSI >80 with negative divergence especially significant ("really hard to get negative RSI divergence with RSI above 80")
4. **EMA cluster failure**: Stock pushes above 5/9/20/50 EMA converged zone then closes back below all of them simultaneously — treated as high-conviction reversal signal
5. **Historic statistical extremes**: RSI at 2nd or 3rd most overbought/oversold all-time; consecutive close streaks beyond historical norms (9 straight weeks = <1–2% chance of continuing)
6. **Bull trap patterns**: Breakout above a prior level or pattern that immediately fails back below — "false breakout reversal" is a recurring trigger phrase
7. **VIX setup**: VIX falling wedge breakout, VIX positive RSI divergence at oversold extremes, VIX reclaiming EMAs
8. **Sector/leader divergence**: Leaders like NVDA making back-to-back red weeks while SPY pushes higher; MAGS not confirming new ATHs; IWM/DJI diverging from SPY/QQQ
9. **Corroborating instruments**: DXY bullish + HYG bearish + Energy (XLE) bullish = bearish for SPY; DAX/Nikkei/HSI rolling over before SPX
10. **Bear flag / ascending wedge / symmetrical triangle breakdowns** on 5min, 15min, 1H, 4H, and daily timeframes — used as continuation and entry-timing signals

## Entry Rules

- **Does not enter on first touch of resistance** — waits for reversal confirmation (e.g., bearish candle close below resistance, not merely a wick)
- **Waits for daily close confirmation**: "close below 5/9ema to short" is the standard he references; weekly candle closes matter for weekly setups
- **Bear flag breakdown used for entry timing**: Enters on breakdown below consolidation after initial reversal, not at the top reversal candle itself (e.g., "$SPY bear flag breakdown and downside continuation" after holding puts from prior day)
- **Higher timeframe elements must support the trade**: "I could have prematurely sold my puts there but higher timeframe elements favored the bear flag leading to downside continuation"
- **Does not enter if only one element present**: Explicitly states setups need multiple bearish elements stacking simultaneously
- **No chasing**: Does not buy breakouts into parabolic moves; explicitly mocks those who do
- **Frequency is very low**: "My last 2 trades were alerted May 7th and May 12th" over a multi-week period — waits for strong signals, not constant activity

- **Bull flag breakouts at extreme overbought**: "$AVGO bull flag breakout into new ATHs. It's what most people see. It's so simple until it fails and you lose your money. It's like crossing the street but not checking both ways"

- **Waiting for earnings to clear before shorting**: "The most annoying thing I need to listen to is someone tell me, wait to short until after earnings. As if the market will wait for us at major ATH resistance until right after earnings week is clear to initiate sell program"
- **Using RSI as a standalone overbought signal to short mid-parabola**: Acknowledges "RSI is overbought but it can stay overbought" is true during extensions — requires ADDITIONAL confirming elements
- **Monthly-timeframe FOMO buying**: "On the monthly time frame, we have not dipped yet. Not even touching 5ema. If you are buying in this timeframe, you are buying the absolute fomo highs"
- **Treating bullish engulfing at ATHs same as at bottoms**: "Bullish engulfing at all time highs isn't the same as bullish engulfing (reversal) candle at the bottom of a downtrend"
