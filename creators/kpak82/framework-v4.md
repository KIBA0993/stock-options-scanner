## Trading Personality
Contrarian reversal trader who waits for extreme conditions and multiple timeframe confluence. Highly technical, chart-pattern focused, and patient — publicly documents waiting weeks between trades. Warns repeatedly against chasing moves, shorting after large drops, or buying first dips after reversals. Educational tone with emphasis on probabilities, rare events, and historical context. Explicitly avoids trolling bulls or bears.

## Market Conditions They Trade
- Extreme oversold/overbought conditions on daily/weekly/monthly RSI
- After historic rallies that create distance from moving averages (50ema, 200ema)
- Major channel range tops/bottoms (multi-year, lifetime channels)
- Multiple timeframe bearish/bullish reversals at key resistance/support
- VIX at extremes (specific: RSI at "FAAO oversold level")
- 9+ consecutive weeks in same direction (statistically rare)
- After "once in a lifetime" parabolic moves

## Market Conditions They Avoid
- Immediately after major reversals ("You always want to take a trade/setup that you would take at least 9 out of 10 times")
- During historic melt-ups where technicals become "useless" (acknowledges this explicitly)
- When extreme momentum continues ("You never want to bet on the side of extreme")
- After assets already down 20%+ in days ("No, you shouldn't short it now")
- When leaders show divergent strength (e.g., NVDA red while SPY green)
- During unprecedented events (e.g., SPCX IPO: "I'll be watching from the sidelines. Exiting all my long positions")

## Setup Triggers — What Gets Their Attention
- **RSI extremes**: Daily RSI at multi-year highs/lows (e.g., "1H RSI is 15. Most oversold in 2 years")
- **Channel patterns**: Lifetime, multi-decade channel tops/bottoms (e.g., "top of 53yr channel range")
- **Moving average confluences**: Price testing multiple EMAs simultaneously (5/9/20/50/200)
- **Reversal candles**: Bearish engulfing, shooting stars, hammers, dojis at extremes
- **Consecutive closes**: 9+ straight weeks same direction, 7+ daily closes
- **Pattern breakdowns/breakouts**: Bull/bear flags, wedges, H&S, diamonds
- **Negative divergences**: RSI not confirming new price highs/lows
- **Gap behavior**: Multiple gap ups/downs signaling exhaustion
- **Relative weakness**: Leaders showing weakness while indices at ATH
