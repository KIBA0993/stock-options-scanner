## Trading Personality
Pure technician, evidence-based. Treats every setup as a probability calculation grounded in chart structure. Fiercely contrarian — most bullish at bottoms when fear is extreme, most bearish at tops when euphoria is rampant. Transparent about losses (posts them publicly). Uses personal accountability penalties (pushups/pullups as wagers on conviction calls). Combative with trolls but this doesn't affect trading discipline.

## Market Conditions They Trade
- Parabolic moves hitting historical multi-decade channel resistance (30yr, 40yr, 50yr channels)
- RSI at extreme overbought/oversold on multiple timeframes simultaneously
- Multiple timeframe confluence: monthly + weekly + daily + 4H all confirming the same direction
- Historic streak events (9+ consecutive weeks higher, 13+ straight daily closes) — treats statistical rarity as a warning
- Market leader divergence: $NVDA or $MAGS failing while $SPX makes new ATHs

## Market Conditions They Avoid
- News-driven moves: "The news is just entertainment" — ignores headlines for entries
- Chopping/sideways markets ("mind-numbing") — waits patiently on the sideline
- Buying breakouts at extreme highs after parabolic runs: "Dont chase extreme overbought"
- Shorting in the hole (doesn't short into already-extreme oversold)
- Boredom trades or weak setups: "Every trade you take that has 'small' or 'lotto' attached to it means you are not taking trading seriously"

## Setup Triggers — What Gets Their Attention
1. **Multi-decade channel resistance** — When $SPX, $QQQ, or sector ETFs hit 30–50 year channel tops where they have failed before
2. **RSI negative divergence at tops** — Price makes new ATH but RSI lower than prior high; more powerful when RSI is above 80 (most overbought in years)
3. **VIX + SPX RSI divergence** — $VIX positive RSI divergence while $SPX has negative RSI divergence = market likely topped for the day
4. **Bull trap confirmation** — Bull flag breakout that immediately fails back below the breakout level
5. **EMA cascade failure** — Multiple EMAs (5/9/20/50) losing support simultaneously in a cluster
6. **Historic statistical rarity** — 9 straight weeks up, 13 straight daily closes, RSI at all-time highs for an index = fat tail warning

## Preferred Instruments & Timeframes
- **Calls vs puts preference:** Primarily puts (reversal/top trader); goes long at bottoms with same conviction
- **Typical DTE range:** Not explicitly stated, but swing positions appear to hold 3–14 days based on timeline of posts
- **Favored underlyings:** $SPY $SPX $ES_F for index plays; $NVDA $TSLA $AAPL $AMD $MU for individual stock options
- **Timeframe:** Swing trades (not 0DTE) — uses daily + weekly for direction, 4H and 1H for entry timing
- **EMA system:** 5/9/20/50 EMA stack. 5EMA = momentum; 9EMA = short-term trend; 20EMA/SMA = medium; 50EMA/SMA = swing

## Entry Rules
1. Wait for bearish **reversal candle** at resistance — not just "we're at resistance"
2. Preferred candle hierarchy (explicitly ranked): **doji > spinning top > hanging man** — "Dojis have a much higher success rate as a reversal candle than shooting stars"
3. **4H candle close** as minimum confirmation for swing entries
4. Green-to-red intraday action at major resistance = early signal
5. Does NOT wait for close below 5/9 EMA if the channel/RSI setup is strong enough — enters at resistance directly
6. Posts extensively before major moves (often 5–10 chart setups preceding a conviction call)

## Exit Rules
- **Scaling exits in quarters:** "All out remaining 1/4 at +380%" — takes partial profits throughout the move
- **Holds runners** when higher timeframe pattern (bear flag, channel breakdown) still supports the thesis: "I could have prematurely sold my puts there but higher timeframe elements favored the bear flag"
- **Closes when RSI reaches extreme oversold** on 4H: "$ES_F 4H RSI extreme oversold — super risky to hold short now"
- **Salvages expiring positions** by rolling when thesis still valid: "salvaged the previous position which was expiring next week"
- No explicit percentage stop mentioned publicly — likely managed via position size

## Risk Management
- **Trade frequency:** Extremely low. "My last 2 trades were alerted May 7th and May 12th — I don't trade that much as I wait for strong signals"
- Explicit rule against boredom trades: had a personal rule "if I enter a boredom trade, I cannot trade the next day"
- **Position sizing:** Uses public feed + private newsletter — public positions are smaller/commentary, private alerts are actual swing trades
- Doesn't overtrade during choppy/sideways markets — stays flat for weeks at a time

## Red Flags — What They Explicitly Avoid
- "Dont be an idiot and buy a bull flag breakout at the top of a parabolic rally"
- "If you need to say 'small' or 'lotto' on a trade, you're taking a weak setup because you can't wait for a strong one"
- "Dont chase extreme overbought. Can always wait for better entry after pullback"
- Buying the first shallow dip off ATHs: "You 100% should not have bought the first shallow dip coming off reversal at ATHs"
- News-driven FOMO: "Market moves and the news doesnt matter — even if you knew what was going to happen ahead of time, it would not have mattered"
- Over-trading during low-signal environments: goes silent for 1–2 weeks between setups
