### Transferable Methodology (Non-Crypto)

The following visual analysis techniques from MasterPandaWu's approach apply directly to US stock options and equity indices:

**1. Confluence-Based Entry Zones**
- Identify 3+ technical factors converging at same price level (Fibonacci retracement, gap fill, trendline, psychological round number)
- Wait for price to reach confluence before entering counter-trend positions
- Example: "6820-30 is 50% retrace, gap-fill zone, AND downward trendline" = reload zone for puts

**2. Intraday Timeframe Precision**
- Primary analysis on 5-minute and 10-minute charts for SPX/SPY
- Allows identification of intraday inflection points and trap patterns
- Uses 2-hour charts for intermediate structure ("Super-Dome" symmetry)
- Daily charts for larger wave counts and measured moves

**3. Gap Behavior Classification**
- Opening gaps after impulsive moves often don't fill immediately (3rd wave gaps)
- Small, quickly-recovered gaps signal weakness in the counter-trend ("opening gap was so small and short-lived, which already cast doubt on ultra-bearish theory")
- Large unfilled gaps during rallies signal strength vs. overlapping candles during declines

**4. Event-Driven Pattern Recognition**
- Identifies "foreplay" vs. "real move" around scheduled events (FOMC, earnings, presidential pressers)
- Pre-event consolidation followed by post-event directional acceleration
- Time-specific inflection points (e.g., "tmrw near 11:00am" for selloff, "Powell Factor at 2:30pm")

**5. Trap-and-Fade Methodology**
- After sharp moves, expect reflexive bounces that trap late participants
- "First shake off the later bears who piled up, then kill the dip-buyers"
- Fade counter-trend bounces at resistance with targets at prior support zones
- Requires identifying which group is being trapped at each stage

**6. VIX Divergence Analysis**
- Price down + VIX down = fear subsiding = potential local bottom
- Used for short-term mean reversion scalps (2-3 day call options)
- Combines with multi-year support zones on major stocks (DJI, NVDA, MSFT)

**7. Asymmetry Analysis**
- Slow, choppy, overlapping declines = corrective (not impulsive)
- Fast, gap-filled advances = impulsive trend moves
- "680 points over 42 sessions with overlapping candles" vs. "530 points in 8 sessions with unfilled gaps"
- Asymmetry confirms primary trend direction

**8. Failed Pattern Recognition**
- Breaking above key resistance doesn't guarantee continuation
- "If 6800 is broken to the upside, then it would signal a large W-3 to all-time highs"
- Establishes clear invalidation levels (X marks on charts)
- Breakout failures become high-conviction reversal trades
